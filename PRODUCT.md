# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is hiring managers, design-forward clients, and potential collaborators who are evaluating Harri Halonen's taste, thinking, and execution. They typically arrive to assess past work quickly, understand the level of craft behind the case studies, and decide whether Harri is someone they want to contact for a role, project, or conversation.

## Product Purpose

This site exists to present Harri Halonen as a thoughtful experience designer and builder through a compact set of memorable project stories. It should make the quality of the work and the person behind it legible fast, then convert interest into outreach through email or calendar booking. Success looks like visitors understanding Harri's perspective, trusting the quality of his craft, and feeling enough affinity and confidence to start a conversation.

## Brand Personality

Thoughtful, warm, quietly confident. The voice should feel human, observant, and self-assured without sounding performative or sales-driven. The emotional goal is to create trust and curiosity through clarity, craft, and a sense of personal presence.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a generic SaaS landing page, a cold corporate consulting site, or an over-animated creative-developer portfolio that values tricks over comprehension. Avoid template-like credibility theater, inflated claims, and visual noise that distracts from the work itself.

## Design Principles

1. Let the work carry the persuasion: case studies, artifacts, and project framing should do more than abstract self-description.
2. Balance personality with restraint: the site should feel unmistakably personal without becoming messy, ironic, or over-designed.
3. Make credibility effortless: visitors should understand Harri's strengths, process, and fit quickly, without needing to decode the interface.
4. Keep the narrative human: the portfolio should show a real person with taste and judgment, not a faceless list of deliverables.
5. Reward deeper attention: the first scan should feel clear, while closer reading should reveal care, nuance, and thoughtful craft.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for strong baseline accessibility with readable typography, dependable contrast, visible focus states, and clear interaction cues. Motion should stay purposeful and restrained, with a bias toward comfort rather than spectacle. Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning, and preserve clarity across a wide range of devices and viewing conditions.
